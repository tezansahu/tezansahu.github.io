ETHIndia Hackathon

My 1st Hackathon Experience!

August 10-12


It was back in January 2018 when I first heard of something called blockchain and its association with cryptography. This caught my fancy and I started reading about cryptocurrencies and blockchains frequently. After successfully completing a Blockchain Fundamentals course in June 2018, I came across a Facebook post about ETHIndia Hackathon - Asia's biggest Ethereum Hackathon. Seeing a lineup of some really interesting people as the speakers and judges, I was keen on getting an opportunity to meet them and explore more into the field of Blockchains. I decided to take part in this event and was overjoyed when my application was accepted. The next challenge was to find someone from IIT Bombay with whom I could team up. It was then when I realized that Akash Kumar and Deep Karman Pal Singh had also been accepted. So, we decided to hack together.

The Preparatory Phase...

The problem statement stated that we had to hack to build something on Ethereum (it could be anything...a game, a DApp, another framework, anything!). In the short span of 1 week that we had before the hackathon, I started looking at DApp (Decentralized App) Development tutorial and familiarized myself with frameworks like Truffle, Ganache-CLI, etc. and also learnt Solidity to write Smart Contracts. Since the problem statement was completely open-ended, we discussed about relevant fields where DApps could make an impact.

On Thursday night, I boarded the flight to Banglore for my 1st hackathon!

Day 0: The Tech-Talks...

It was around 8:00 am when we reached MLR Convention Centre. During the Check-In process and breakfast, we had a chat with the organizers and several participants from India and abroad.

Day 0 kicked off with the organizers introducing the sponsors and their contributions to the blockchain space. What followed, was a series of great talks and short workshops by people from organizations including Status, Lendroid, Matic Network, Consensys, MakerDAO, Dharma Labs and many more...


The lunch and high-tea served as good opportunities to talk to the speakers. The talks and interactions helped us understand the humongous amount of development that had sparked off in this field over the last few years and started giving us a broader picture about Blockchains and their applications.
